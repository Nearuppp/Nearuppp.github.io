Maze Robot Electronics and Wiring

2.2.1 General Structure The electronic system forms the core of our robot’s control mechanism. It includes a microcontroller, continuous rotation servomotors, distance sensors, and a battery, all essential for the robot’s autonomous operation. Key Components: Microcontroller (Maker Nano): Executes the code and controls various peripherals, including servomotors and sensors. Servomotors: Drive the robot’s wheels, offering a balance of low power consumption and adequate speed (around 100 RPM at 4.8V). Distance Sensors (VL6180X): Provide millimeter-precision measurements up to 20 cm, crucial for navigating the maze....

Navigation Strategy

When the center sensor detects an obstacle more than 14 cm away: The robot can choose to move forward or turn slightly left or right. To move forward, the left and right sensors must detect obstacles at least 8 cm away. When the center sensor detects an obstacle within 14 cm: The robot adjusts its speed and direction to avoid obstacles using a variable calculated by the “map” command, which refines movement based on sensor data....
